The math, in the open

Every number has a source. Audit the assumptions, swap in your own.

Lapse rate · 9% (conservative)
Industry benchmarks range 9–25% depending on source.Dental Intelligence's 4,000-practice panel reports 25% annual attrition; Overjet/Clerri industry aggregate is 17%; Henry Schein One's benchmark shows 57% average retention (i.e. ~43% five-year loss). Our default of 9% measures annual lapse (recall-overdue, still listed active), which is a narrower metric than attrition. We deliberately pick the conservative end for the calculator.
Per-visit production · $285 (blended)
Planet DDS 2025 Dental Industry Outlook (3,400-practice panel).$285 is our blended average per-visit estimate across hygiene + diagnostic + restorative. Hygiene-visit production specifically averages $471 (eAssist). Average daily gross production is $8,436 per practice. Your practice's number is likely between $225–$400 depending on payer mix.
Reactivation rate · 8–10%
Multi-touch 81% lift over single-touch (Levin Group).Industry typical reactivation campaign converts ~10% of an inactive list (Marketly). Phone-added campaigns lift recall return by up to 55% (eAssist). In our own 12-practice pilot cohort, 5-touch (email + SMS + phone + email + SMS) runs at 12.8% vs. 1.7% for a single SMS. Full dataset: github.com/colin330smith/usdpr-benchmarks.
Visits per reactivated patient · 1.5×
One hygiene + partial restorative.$520 average short-term value per reactivated patient (CallForce via DentistryIQ). Full patient LTV averages $12,000–$15,000 over the relationship (eTactics, Clerri). 1.5× visits in the first 12 months is the conservative slice.
Why reactivation matters now · 2026
35% of US dentists are "not busy enough" — highest in 3 years.Source: ADA Health Policy Institute Q3 2025. Simultaneously, 22% of patient bases were affected by insurance network changes in late 2024 (ADA HPI, Dec 2024), and 30.1% of practices dropped some insurance networks.
