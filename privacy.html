§ 01

Who we are.

US Dental Patient Recovery ("USDPR," "we," "us") is an orthodontic-focused patient recovery software operated by USDPR LLC, a Florida limited-liability company based in Orlando, FL. Founder: Colin Smith. Sole operator at publication. Contact details are at the bottom of this document.

This privacy notice governs the personal information we collect through usdentalpatientrecovery.com, our marketing emails, and our customer-facing product dashboards. Protected health information (PHI) we process as a HIPAA Business Associate on behalf of customer practices is governed separately by the signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and by §04 of this notice.

§ 02

Two kinds of data. Different rules for each.

We keep a clean separation between two data domains. This separation is not just stylistic — it's load-bearing, and it governs what we can and can't do with each.

  • Marketing data. Your name, work email, practice name, phone number, website analytics, calculator submissions, newsletter subscriptions. Governed by this privacy notice and ordinary US privacy law.
  • Patient data. PHI — protected health information of the patients seen by our customer practices. We never touch a byte of this until a Business Associate Agreement is signed. Governed by HIPAA, the BAA, and §04 below.
§ 03

Marketing data — website & email.

What we collect

  • Contact information you give us: email, name, practice name, phone, role.
  • Content you submit: recall-audit calculator inputs, pilot application answers, support tickets.
  • Usage data: pages viewed, links clicked, approximate IP-derived geography, browser/device, referrer. Collected through a first-party analytics tool; no third-party advertising cookies.
  • Email engagement: opens, clicks, bounces, unsubscribes (through Resend).

Why we collect it

  • To deliver the audit, whitepaper, or calculator result you asked for.
  • To send you follow-up emails about the pilot — only while you're actively opted in.
  • To measure which pages convert and which fail, so we can build a better product.
  • To comply with tax, accounting, and anti-fraud obligations.

What we do not do

We do not sell your contact information. We do not share it with data brokers. We do not run third-party retargeting pixels (no Meta pixel, no Google Ads pixel, no LinkedIn insight tag on this site). We do not auto-enroll you in marketing sequences you didn't ask for.

Legal basis

Under US consumer-privacy law generally, and applicable state laws (CCPA/CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, UCPA where you reside), we process marketing data under the basis of performance of contract or legitimate interest with opt-out available at any time. EU/UK residents: see privacy@usdentalpatientrecovery.com for your specific rights.

§ 04

Patient data — PHI under BAA.

When a customer practice signs a BAA with USDPR and connects their practice management system (Open Dental, Dentrix, Cloud 9, Ortho2), USDPR becomes a HIPAA Business Associate of that practice under 45 CFR §160.103.

Minimum necessary

We access only the data fields required to compute Recovery Opportunity states — per 45 CFR §164.502(b). Specifically, we read: patient identifiers (name, DOB, phone, email), appointment history, scheduled/completed/missed status, treatment phase codes, responsible-party linkage for family groupings. We do not read: SSNs, insurance account numbers, clinical notes, imaging, financial ledgers.

What we do with it

  • Classify each patient into one of the eight Recovery Opportunity states.
  • Rank opportunities by probability × expected shown-value.
  • Deliver a weekly Friday call list to the customer's designated staff via email.
  • Track shown-patient attribution against the list.

What we do not do

We do not sell, lease, license, or otherwise transfer PHI to any third party. We do not use PHI to train general AI models. We do not market to patients directly — our outputs are delivered exclusively to the contracting practice.

AI reply triage (disclosure)

With the customer's opt-in, we process inbound SMS reply text (from patients to the customer's messaging number) through Anthropic's Claude API under a BAA with Anthropic. Only the reply text, a numeric patient ID, and the triage category (positive / reschedule request / complaint / opt-out) are exchanged. Anthropic operates under its own BAA; inputs are not used for model training. Customers who decline this feature receive a manual triage inbox instead.

De-identification for product improvement

We may derive de-identified aggregated statistics (e.g., "average retainer-check overdue ratio across all practices = 18%") per Safe Harbor under 45 CFR §164.514(b)(2). De-identified statistics cannot reasonably be used to re-identify any patient and may be published in our marketing or benchmarks without practice consent beyond what's already in the BAA.

§ 05

Sub-processors.

USDPR uses a small, auditable set of sub-processors. Each is either (a) covered by a signed BAA where PHI flows, or (b) restricted to non-PHI marketing data. Current list:

  • Amazon Web Services (us-east-1). BAA in place. Primary infrastructure — compute, database, storage, KMS key management.
  • Anthropic. BAA in place. Reply-text classification; opt-in only.
  • Twilio. BAA in place. SMS delivery for customer outbound messaging; 10DLC-registered.
  • Resend. No BAA; marketing-email delivery only. No PHI transits Resend.
  • Stripe. No BAA; payment processing only. Practice billing contact info only. No PHI.
  • Netlify / Vercel. No BAA; static-site and marketing-form hosting only. No PHI.

We maintain a current sub-processor register at /security. Material changes to the PHI-bearing list are notified to customers at least 30 days in advance.

§ 06

Retention & deletion.

Marketing data. We retain contact information for as long as you're in active communication plus 24 months of inactivity, then we purge. You can request deletion at any time via the unsubscribe footer or privacy@usdentalpatientrecovery.com.

Patient data (PHI). Retained for the duration of the BAA. Upon BAA termination, we return or destroy all PHI per 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(J) within 30 days, with a signed attestation of destruction where return is infeasible. Customers may request destruction logs on request.

Backups. Encrypted database backups are retained for 35 days and rotate out automatically.

§ 07

Your rights.

Depending on where you reside, you may have some or all of the following rights over your marketing data held by USDPR:

  • Right to know what personal information we have collected about you
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to delete personal information (subject to legal retention)
  • Right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell or share)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising any of the above

Rights over PHI — including access, amendment, accounting of disclosures — are exercised through your treating practice (the HIPAA "covered entity"), not through USDPR directly. We will support the practice's fulfillment of any such request within five business days.

To exercise marketing-data rights, email privacy@usdentalpatientrecovery.com. We respond within 45 days. We do not require you to create an account to exercise these rights, and we do not charge a fee.

§ 08

Security posture.

  • All data in transit is protected by TLS 1.3.
  • All data at rest is encrypted with AWS KMS-managed keys; PHI-bearing volumes use customer-managed-key isolation.
  • Access to production is limited to the founder and requires hardware security key + 2FA.
  • All production access is logged; logs are retained 12 months and are available to customers on request.
  • We do not export PHI to local developer machines. Ever.
  • SOC 2 Type II is on the Q2 2027 roadmap. Pre-audit posture is published at /security.
§ 09

Breach notification.

Should an incident affect the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of PHI, we notify the affected customer-practice in writing without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 24 hours from detection — faster than the HIPAA floor of 60 days at 45 CFR §164.410. We support the customer's notification obligations with forensic timelines, affected-record counts, and regulator-ready disclosures.

For marketing-data incidents, we follow the applicable US state breach-notification statutes where you reside.

§ 10

Children's data.

Many orthodontic patients are minors. PHI about minor patients flows through USDPR only under the BAA with the treating practice, with all the same protections described in §04. We do not directly collect personal information from children through our marketing website or emails, and our product is not offered to end-users under 13.
