FAQ

What dentists actually ask.

Is this legal in my state?

In all 50 states, yes — because we only contact your existing patients, under your practice's name. That's follow-up care, not patient solicitation.

Example: Florida Rule 64B5-4.002 explicitly exempts this. We charge a flat fee, never per-patient — which keeps the arrangement inside the Florida Patient Brokering Act safe harbor and equivalent anti-kickback statutes elsewhere.

If your state has unusual restrictions, your onboarding email includes a state-specific compliance summary before any outreach begins.

What about HIPAA?

We sign a Business Associate Agreement before you send us any patient data. No exceptions.

Every text passes a PHI scrubber. Every send, reply, and booking is logged. Data sits on encrypted volumes. If your attorney wants a walkthrough, I can do that in an hour.

What does my front desk actually do?

Four things. 1. Export your overdue list (30 minutes, once). 2. Approve our templates before we send. 3. Receive warm handoffs when patients are ready to book. 4. Tell us who showed up and who didn't for the Friday report.

What if it doesn't work?

You cancel with seven days' notice and keep your data. No per-patient invoice, no cancellation fee. If the report isn't showing movement by week three, I'll tell you to cancel before you ask.

Why flat fee and not pay-per-rebook?

Legal: per-patient payments in Florida healthcare are a third-degree felony. Operational: pay-per-rebook rewards pushing patients who shouldn't be pushed. Accounting: flat means no Friday arguments about attribution.

How fast can I actually start?

Checkout takes under 90 seconds. BAA auto-signs via DocuSign in the email that follows. Upload your CSV when you're ready (there's a walkthrough). Typical practice is live in 48 hours — first outreach going out by the third business day.

What dental software do you work with?

Dentrix, Eaglesoft, and Open Dental are the three I have written export guides for. Any PMS that produces a CSV with patient name, phone, email, and last-visit date works.
