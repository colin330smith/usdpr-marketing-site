The real problem

Your practice doesn't have a lead problem. It has a chart-attrition problem — and it's quietly bleeding visits you've already earned.

Every orthodontic practice carries 696 active-treatment patients on average, plus an observation file of pre-treatment kids, plus a consult pipeline of un-started patients, plus a retention cohort of post-debond retainer checks. One missed prompt and any of them lapse.

The industry's playbook has been to run SMS blasts through a general communication tool and hope. That works for GP dental. It doesn't work for ortho — because ortho recovery isn't one thing. It's eight specific patient states, each with its own trigger, script, and attribution.

That's what USDPR was built to do.

Canon §05 · The hard questions

Objections, answered in plain text.

Q.

We already run Weave / Solutionreach / Dental Intel. What do you add?

Those are general communication platforms designed for GP dental. They do bulk SMS, recare reminders, and basic reactivation campaigns — well. They do not model the eight ortho-specific patient states, they do not do 4-layer attribution with a matched control group, and they do not hand your TC a ranked Friday call list. We're complementary, not a replacement: keep Weave for front-desk comms, add USDPR for the recovery pipeline.

Q.

Is this HIPAA-compliant?

Yes. We operate under a signed Business Associate Agreement before a single row of PHI moves. We follow 45 CFR §164.502 minimum-necessary standard: we access only the fields required to identify the Recovery Opportunity state. No SSNs, no financials, no clinical notes. All transport is TLS 1.3; all storage is encrypted at rest with AWS KMS-managed keys. SOC 2 Type II on the 2027 Q2 roadmap.

Q.

Can you prove patients actually showed up — not just that SMS got sent?

Yes. That's the whole product. Every Recovery Opportunity is tracked through four layers: Detected (we identified the state from your PMS), Scheduled (your TC booked them), Shown (they physically arrived), and Incremental (compared against a matched hold-out cohort inside your chart who never received outreach). Your quarterly CFO certificate is based on Shown, not Detected.

Q.

Why ortho-only? Why not dental in general?

Because the recovery math is different. A GP patient with a missed hygiene is worth $180–$280 recovered. An ortho observation patient who starts treatment at the right window is worth $5,108–$9,221 gross2. An ortho retainer-check-overdue patient who ends up in relapse is worth $3k–$7k of refinement. The unit economics demand a dedicated product — and no general-purpose dental tool models the eight ortho states correctly.

Q.

What if we have a small active-chart? Does this work under 400 patients?

The Recovery Launch will still produce value, but the monthly Pro may not pencil cleanly below ~350 active-treatment patients. We'll tell you this on the pre-pilot call. For smaller practices we recommend starting with just the Recovery Launch, running the guarantee period, and deciding on monthly from there.
