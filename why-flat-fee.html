Compliance · the legal architecture

Why we price flat. And publish the memo.

Every dental marketing vendor that charges per patient, per booking, or per referral is living inside a legal trap. Federal anti-kickback safe harbors, state patient-brokering statutes, and HHS OIG advisory opinions as recent as 2025 have all tightened on per-transaction fees for patient-facing healthcare marketing. USDPR prices a flat monthly SaaS fee — and publishes the Fair Market Value memo your compliance officer will ask for.

The short version

Per-patient pricing is illegal or legally fragile in most US states for dental marketing. Flat-fee SaaS is the compliant alternative, and USDPR documents it with a published FMV memo and a stricter-than-industry HIPAA BAA.

  • Federal 42 U.S.C. §1320a-7b (Anti-Kickback Statute) — per-transaction fees outside the safe harbors in 42 CFR §1001.952 are prohibited.
  • Florida §817.505 (Patient Brokering) — criminalizes per-patient referral compensation in healthcare settings.
  • California Business & Professions Code §650 — same.
  • HHS OIG Advisory Opinion 25-08 (2025) — clarified that flat-fee alone is not sufficient; the fee must reflect genuine software value independent of volume. USDPR's FMV memo documents this.
  • Connecticut Attorney General settlement (2024) + Texas OAG enforcement (2025) — dental marketing vendors punished for per-referral pricing ($116/referral in one case).
USDPR policy

We charge $1,500 up front (refundable if guarantee misses) and $697–$1,497 per month, full stop.

The monthly fee does not vary with how many patients are contacted, how many appointments are booked, or how much revenue is recovered. An empty practice pays the same as a full one. That is what makes our pricing defensible under the legal architecture.

The FMV memo

Every paying USDPR practice receives a 2-page Fair Market Value memorandum on request, suitable for attachment to the practice's compliance binder. It documents:

  1. The software's function (multi-channel reactivation sequencing with AI-assisted reply handling)
  2. The market comparables (published pricing for Weave, Solutionreach, RevenueWell, Adit, Dental Intelligence as of the quarter of purchase)
  3. Why our monthly fee reflects the economic value of software + infrastructure independent of patient volume or bookings generated
  4. Signed by the USDPR founder with a commitment to re-issue annually if pricing changes

This is the document HHS OIG AO 25-08 (2025) effectively requires for any vendor that wants flat-fee compliance to hold up under enforcement scrutiny. No competitor publishes one.

HIPAA — our BAA is stricter than the industry

USDPR's Business Associate Agreement is public at /security.html and includes commitments competitors don't:

  • No external AI training on PHI. Competitor BAAs (Weave's is public) permit de-identification and external AI use. Ours forbids both.
  • No PHI resale. Explicit contractual ban on sharing de-identified aggregate data with third parties for commercial purposes.
  • 72-hour breach notification. The HIPAA statutory window is 60 days. We commit contractually to 72 hours.
  • PMS-level minimum-necessary. We pull only lapsed-patient fields (name, email, mobile, last-visit date, provider-of-record). Clinical fields (procedure codes, notes, diagnoses, ICD-10, CDT) stay inside the PMS.

SMS — strictest-state by default

Our SMS engine hard-codes the strictest overlap of current US state rules:

  • Quiet hours: 9am–8pm Monday–Saturday, noon–8pm Sunday, local-to-patient time zone
  • Volume cap: 3 SMS per recipient per 24 hours, 5 per 7 days
  • STOP handling: unsubscribe honored within 2 minutes, not the TCPA 10-day grace period. Cross-campaign: a STOP to one sequence stops all future sends across every campaign (April 2026 FCC rule compliance)
  • 10DLC carrier registration: Every practice gets its own registered brand (not pooled), tied to its LLC

These limits satisfy the Florida Telephone Solicitation Act, Texas SB 140, California Business & Professions Code §17538.41, and the April 2026 FCC cross-campaign opt-out rule — without per-state configuration by the practice.
