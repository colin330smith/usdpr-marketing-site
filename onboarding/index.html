1. Definitions

"Protected Health Information" (PHI) has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §160.103. "Covered Entity" means the dental practice named below. "Business Associate" means US Dental Patient Recovery LLC ("USDPR").

2. Permitted Uses & Disclosures

USDPR may use or disclose PHI only to provide the reactivation services described in the Subscription Agreement, carry out legal responsibilities, or perform data-aggregation services for the Covered Entity, consistent with 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164.

3. Prohibited Uses (stricter than HIPAA baseline)

USDPR expressly agrees not to: (a) use PHI to train any external AI, machine-learning, or large language model; (b) de-identify PHI for any purpose outside direct service of the Covered Entity; (c) sell, license, or transfer PHI (identified or de-identified) to any third party for commercial purposes; (d) combine PHI with any external data source except as explicitly authorized by the Covered Entity.

4. Safeguards

Encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). All PHI access logged in an append-only audit trail with 7-year retention, per 45 CFR Part 164 Subpart C.

5. Breach Notification

USDPR will notify the Covered Entity of any Breach of Unsecured PHI within 72 hours of discovery, stricter than the HIPAA statutory 60-day window.

6. Subcontractors

USDPR requires any subcontractor receiving PHI to execute a BAA with provisions substantially similar to this Agreement. Current subcontractors: Resend (email), Twilio (SMS), hosting provider, domain-restricted Anthropic API (reply classification, inference only — no PHI in training).

7. Termination

On termination, USDPR will return or destroy all PHI within 30 calendar days, certified in writing. If infeasible, protections of this BAA extend to retained PHI.

8. Governing Law

State of Florida + applicable federal healthcare statutes and regulations. ESIGN Act of 2000 governs electronic signature.
