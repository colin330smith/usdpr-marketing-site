Dental practice transition — how to retain patients through ownership, location, or staff change

20-35% of patients leave during a practice transition by default. With the playbook below, that number drops to 5-10%.

Why transitions lose patients

Three reasons, in order: uncertainty (patients don't know if their trusted dentist is still there), friction (new log-in systems, new forms, new location), and drift (they meant to come back but never did).

Baseline: 25-30% of patients churn within 12 months of a practice transition. Worst-case: 40-50% (associate buys out a retiring dentist without overlap). Best-case: 5-10% with proper communication.

Playbook for ownership transition (Dr. X buys out Dr. Y)

Week -4 to -1 (before transition): - Joint letter from both doctors introducing the new owner. - 90-day overlap where old owner still sees long-time patients. Eases transfer of trust.

Week 0 (transition day): - Second letter. Recap the team, who's staying, what's the same. - Welcome video from new doctor on the website.

Month 1-3: - Personal call to all 'high-LTV long-tenured' patients (5+ years, $3k+/year production). - Free continuation-of-care visit offer for patients who haven't been in 6+ months.

Month 6-12: - Proactive reactivation campaign for any patient who hasn't visited since transition.

Playbook for location change

10 weeks out: email announcement. 8 weeks out: mailed letter with map + 'here's why we're moving.' 4 weeks out: SMS reminder + 'your next appointment is at the new location.' Move week: photo tour of new office on website + social. Post-move 6 months: proactive reactivation for any patient who hasn't been in. Address change drives 15-20% churn baseline; proactive outreach cuts it to 5-10%.

Playbook for provider change (new associate joins)

New provider = opportunity, not risk, if handled right: - Profile page on website before they start. - Patient introduction email sharing bio, specialty areas, why they joined. - Dr. who hired them warmly cross-referring specific patients: 'Dr. X has advanced training in ___ which would be great for your situation.'

Within 6 months, target 15-20% of patients to have had at least one visit with the new provider. That mix-in reduces single-doctor retention risk.

The common thread: proactive communication

Transitions churn patients by default because uncertainty is the kill switch. Every proactive communication reduces that uncertainty. Under-communicating is the #1 mistake.

Frequently asked

When does a dental practice transition most risk patient loss?

Months 2-6 post-transition, usually because the old dentist is physically gone by then but new patient relationships haven't solidified. Aggressive retention campaigns in months 1-3 reduce this.

Should I re-introduce myself to every patient after buying a practice?

Yes — at minimum via email, ideally a personal call for top 10-15% by production. The ROI is massive; retention jumps 10-15 percentage points.

Do letter + email + SMS during transitions conflict?

No — multi-channel is better during transitions, not worse. Uncertainty is what drives churn, and redundancy of communication reduces uncertainty.
