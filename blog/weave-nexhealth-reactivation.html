Weave vs. NexHealth vs. RevenueWell vs. Solutionreach — the honest 2026 comparison.

9 min read · 2026-04-19

Four dental-SaaS incumbents. All strong at what they bundle. None built specifically for reactivation. This post walks through each one honestly — who they're for, where they win, where they lose — with published 2026 pricing (verified against the vendors' own sites + G2 / Capterra / Software Advice).

Weave

What it is: Phone system + messaging + payments + scheduling + reviews + reactivation, all in one login. $1B+ valuation. The incumbent everyone compares to.

2026 pricing: $249/mo (Pro) starting, Elite / Ultimate / Enterprise custom. Real-world bundled cost with phones typically $500–$900/mo. Annual contract.

Wins:

  • Full phone-system replacement with VoIP hardware
  • 40+ PMS integrations live
  • 200-person support org, 24×7 coverage
  • Brand recognition at every dental conference

Loses:

  • 5–14 day implementation vs. same-day self-serve
  • Reactivation is 1 of 20 features — generic segmentation, generic analytics
  • Published $249 price rarely matches real bundled spend
  • Annual contract locks you in
  • No public demo — sales call gate

Buy Weave if: you're replacing your phone system AND want reactivation AND can wait 2 weeks to start.

NexHealth

What it is: Online booking + PMS integration platform. Modern tech, 50+ PMS integrations, strong in-bound patient-booking UX.

2026 pricing: ~$299–$350/mo starting, enterprise custom. Month-to-month or annual.

Wins:

  • 50+ PMS integrations, most live today
  • Patient-facing online booking UI (we don't do this)
  • API access on standard tier+
  • Modern stack, reasonably fast onboarding (~5–10 days)

Loses:

  • Reactivation is a feature, not the focus
  • Cohort analytics shallow on standard tier
  • Sales call required — no public demo

Buy NexHealth if: online patient booking + PMS-integrated appointment flow is the main pain, not just reactivation.

Full NexHealth-alternative breakdown →

RevenueWell

What it is: Dental marketing + patient communication suite. Henry Schein-owned since 2019. Sold heavily through Patterson channels.

2026 pricing: $189+/mo starting (real-world bundled $400–$700/mo). Typically annual contracts.

Wins:

  • Patient portal + forms + reviews bundled
  • Patterson / Henry Schein ecosystem integration

Loses:

  • Sales motion, not self-serve
  • Reactivation is generic "recall"
  • Annual contract; data-export fee reported
  • Modern UX lacking

Buy RevenueWell if: you're already in the Patterson ecosystem and want the bundled suite.

Full RevenueWell-alternative breakdown →

Solutionreach

What it is: 20-year-old patient-communication suite. Widest feature set; heaviest legacy UX.

2026 pricing: ~$329–$400/mo starting. Annual contracts + auto-renewal by default.

Wins:

  • Most comprehensive feature breadth
  • Strong for large DSOs already invested in the platform

Loses:

  • 14–28 day implementation (widely cited as slow)
  • 2014-era UI, daily usability complaints
  • Annual auto-renewal contracts
  • Cohort analytics enterprise-only

Buy Solutionreach if: you're a large DSO with existing deployment and switching cost dominates.
