Hygiene recall email wording — 9 openers that outperform 'time for your cleaning'

Subject lines + opening paragraphs that beat the default hygiene recall template. Data from 200+ dental practices' email sends.

The 'time for your check-up' problem

Most PMS-shipped recall templates lead with 'Hi {first_name}, time for your {six_month} check-up!' Subject-line open rates hover at 22-28%. Reply rates under 2%. The template is polite, generic, and gets deleted. The 9 openers below run 35-52% open rate and 5-9% reply rate against the same list.

Opener 01 · The lost-benefit angle (Q4 use)

Subject: Dental benefits expire in 62 days — 5-min check?

Body: Hi {first}, short note from Dr. {last}'s office. Most dental benefits zero out on Jan 1 — if you've been paying premiums and haven't used them, they vanish in 62 days. We have hygiene slots open next week if you'd like to claim yours.

Opener 02 · The hygienist-continuity hook

Subject: {hygienist_first} has an opening Thursday

Body: Hi {first}, {hygienist_first} mentioned you hadn't been in for a bit and wondered if you wanted to come in. She has {day} at {time} open.

Opener 03 · The permission-to-opt-out

Subject: Last check-in if you're still a {practice_name} patient

Body: Hi {first}, we haven't heard from you in {X} months. Quick poll: 1) still interested in staying with us — reply YES; 2) no longer needed — reply STOP; 3) busy, maybe later — reply LATER.

Opener 04 · The specific-procedure reminder (HIPAA-safe)

Subject: Your treatment plan has one more step

Body: Dr. {last} noted in your chart that a follow-up was scheduled. If you'd like to come in to complete the plan, we have {day} at {time} open. Reply YES to confirm. Do not name procedure in the email body if sharing sensitive PHI.

Opener 05 · The family-scheduling hook

Subject: Getting {spouse_first} and {kid_first}'s cleanings done — want to come in too?

Body: Hi {first}, we had {family_members} on the books for {month}. Thought we'd offer to add you in the same window if you've been meaning to come in.

Opener 06 · The front-desk first-person

Subject: Quick question from Rachel

Body: Hi {first}, Rachel here from Dr. {last}'s office. I was pulling the schedule for next month and wanted to check if you wanted me to hold time for your cleaning. Was it every 6 or 4 months for you?

Opener 07 · The schedule-hole opportunity

Subject: One opening Wednesday at 2pm

Body: Hi {first}, a cancellation freed up 2pm Wednesday with {hygienist_first}. Wanted to offer it to you first before we post it to the waitlist.

Opener 08 · The non-sales life-update

Subject: Small update from {practice_name}

Body: Hi {first}, wanted to share: we've added {Dr. new_associate_name / new_technology / new_hygienist}. No pressure at all, but if you've been meaning to come back, this is a nice moment.

Opener 09 · The re-engagement after STOP

Only use if the patient previously opted out of SMS but email is still fine. Subject: A quiet email from {practice_name}

Body: Hi {first}, you unsubscribed from our texts a while back (totally respected). This is our last email unless you want to hear from us — just reply YES and we'll keep you on the list, or reply STOP and we won't email again.

Subject-line testing

Open rates vary 2-3× based on subject alone. Run a 50-split for any new opener: send A to 50 patients, B to 50 patients, send the winner to the rest of the list. A 25-patient sample is NOT enough — noise is too high.

Frequently asked

How long should a recall email be?

Under 90 words. Dentists' patients skim. One paragraph, one ask, one button.

Should recall emails include photos?

No. Text-only converts higher, delivers better (less likely to hit promotions tab), and loads on every device.

What's the best day to send recall email?

Tuesday and Thursday 10am-11am local time for highest open rates. Avoid Monday (catch-up) and Friday-Sunday (low engagement).
