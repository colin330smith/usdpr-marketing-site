5 SMS templates that work.

The exact scripts I use to reactivate dental patients. Steal them. Use them in your own PMS. No strings.

TCPA-compliant · STOP + HELP tested · Tap any message to copy
Template 01
7-month overdue hygiene · first touch
Best day/time: Tue 10 AM

Context: patient last seen 6–9 months ago. Still on your roster, SMS consent on file. Warm tone, assumes they just got busy.

Hi [FIRST_NAME] — it's [PRACTICE_NAME]. We noticed it's been a while since your last visit and wanted to check in. No pressure — just making sure you're good. If you'd like to get back on the schedule, here's our calendar: [BOOKING_LINK] Reply STOP to opt out.
Why it works: Name + practice name builds trust in the first 6 words. "No pressure" neutralizes sales-y vibes. Direct booking link removes friction.
Template 02
Broken / cancelled appointment · 2-week follow-up
Best day/time: Wed 2 PM

Context: patient cancelled or no-showed recently. Empathetic, zero guilt-trip. Make it easy to rebook.

Hi [FIRST_NAME] — [PRACTICE_NAME] here. Life happens. Ready to get you back on the books whenever works — we've held a spot for you. Reply with "yes" or pick a time: [BOOKING_LINK] Reply STOP to opt out.
Why it works: "Life happens" removes shame. "Held a spot" creates gentle urgency. Two-path reply (yes or link) catches both conversation and self-serve behaviors.
Template 03
Long-dormant · 18–24 months out
Best day/time: Thu 11 AM

Context: patient gone a year+. They may have switched practices or moved. Re-introduce. Low ask.

Hi [FIRST_NAME]. This is [PRACTICE_NAME] — we still have your chart. If you've found another dentist, no worries and we'll take you off our list (reply STOP). If not, we'd love to see you: [BOOKING_LINK] Reply STOP to opt out.
Why it works: Acknowledges they may have moved on — removes defensiveness. "We still have your chart" is a quiet loyalty signal. Clean opt-out path keeps the list hygienic.
Template 04
Responded "interested" · send the link
Within 10 min of reply

Context: patient said yes / "I'd like to book" / similar. Act fast. Send the link, offer two specific times.

Great — thanks [FIRST_NAME]. Two options: 1. [DATE_OPTION_1] at [TIME_1] 2. [DATE_OPTION_2] at [TIME_2] Or pick any time: [BOOKING_LINK] Reply with 1 or 2 and I'll lock it in.
Why it works: Two specific times > "pick any." Reduces decision fatigue. "Lock it in" language closes faster than "confirm" or "book" — it implies the work is already done for them.
Template 05
Day-before reminder · booked
24h before visit

Context: patient booked, appointment tomorrow. Reminder + easy reschedule. Reduces no-shows by 20–30%.

Hi [FIRST_NAME] — [PRACTICE_NAME]. Confirming your visit tomorrow at [TIME]. Reply "C" to confirm, "R" to reschedule, or stop by: [PRACTICE_ADDRESS]. See you then.
Why it works: Single-letter reply (C or R) is the lowest-friction confirm pattern. Address at the end catches "wait, where?" confusion in one message instead of two.

What to remember before you send

  • SMS consent first. Only send to patients who have affirmatively consented to SMS on intake or in writing. Consent is required under the TCPA.
  • Honor STOP instantly and permanently. A "STOP" reply means never text that number again — even if the patient later says they want you to.
  • Cap at 1 per day, 3 per week per patient. TCPA best practice. Pushing past this is where practices get flagged.
  • No PHI in message bodies. "Time for your visit" is fine. "Time for your crown prep on tooth #19" is not. Ever.
  • Register your 10DLC campaign with your SMS provider (Twilio, etc.) before sending commercial volume. Unregistered SMS gets blocked by carriers starting September 2023.
