RevenueWell vs. usdpr. — the honest version.

RevenueWell (Henry Schein-owned since 2019) is a broad dental-marketing suite: reminders, portal, recall, reviews, two-way texting. If the job is "replace 5 disconnected tools with one," RevenueWell makes sense. If the job is "bring back the patients who've fallen off the schedule" — that's where usdpr. goes deeper.

When RevenueWell is the right call

You want the Patterson ecosystem. Henry Schein / Patterson channels push RevenueWell — your rep will set up everything. If that's your buying motion, fine.

You need a patient portal + forms + reviews + marketing in one login. RevenueWell has all of that. We don't — and won't add them.

When usdpr. is the right call

Reactivation specifically. RevenueWell does it via generic "recall campaigns." We do it via A1/A2/B1/B2/B3 segmentation, cohort analytics by segment × channel × day-of-week, and reply-intent classification (interested / question / objection / unsubscribe).

You hate sales calls. RevenueWell is a sales-first motion. Self-serve demo + instant signup is our default.

Month-to-month, not annual lock-in. RevenueWell typically ships on annual contracts. Every usdpr. tier is cancel-anytime. Annual is optional (17% off) after the first 90 days.

Published pricing. RevenueWell's $189 starting price is real for the entry-tier featureset — but practices report $400–$700/month bundled. Ours is $297 / $697 / $1,997, caps published, no surprises.
